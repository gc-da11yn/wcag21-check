Separating Power

An important feature of our system is the separation and independence of powers among three branches of government: the Legislative Branch, the Executive Branch, and the Judicial Branch.

Branches

Select each branch to learn about their powers.

Learn more about the Legislative Branch

The Legislative Branch, or Parliament, has the power to pass laws, including laws that allow for the spending of money.

Le pouvoir législatif, ou Parlement, a le pouvoir d’édicter des lois, y compris des lois autorisant des dépenses.

Learn more about the Executive Branch

The Executive Branch has the power to propose action and follow through when the related spending is approved.

Le pouvoir exécutif a le pouvoir de proposer des actions et d’y donner suite lorsque les dépenses sont approuvées.

Central Agencies

Central agencies play an important part in the consultations taking place among departments, listening for where there is agreement and trying to resolve disagreements before proposals go to a Cabinet policy committee.
